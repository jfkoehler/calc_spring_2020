Homework¶

  1. Draw a rectangle with two sides that are determined by some arbitrary functions \(h(x)\) and \(w(x)\), that represent the width and height of the figure.

    • Extend each side by a small amount; \(dh\) and \(dw\) respectively.

    • Write a function \(A(x)\) to represent the area of the rectangle in terms of functions \(h\) and \(w\).

    • Compute the value of the larger rectangle

    • How will the value of the corner piece \(dh \times dw\) compare to our others? Do we need it?

    • Make an argument for dismissing this piece, and write your new expression.

    • Use the product rule to find the derivative of \(A(x)\), compare this to your expression above. What’s going on here?

  2. Derivative Practice: For each of the following, find the derivative \(\frac{dy}{dx}\):

  • \(f(x) = x^7 + 10\)

  • \(f(x) = 3x(5x^3 - \frac{10}{x + 3})\)

  1. For the following problems, detemine and evaluate the derivative at given value:

  • \(h(x) = 3xf(x) + 8g(x)\); find \(h'(2)\)

  • \(h(x) = \frac{3}{x} + f(x)g(x)\); find \(h'(1)\)

  1. Use implicit differentiation to find \(\frac{dy}{dx}\)

  • \(3x^3 +9xy^2 = 5x^3\)

  • \(y\sin(xy) = y^2 + 2\)

  1. A rectangular box with a square base, an open top, and a volume of 216 in.3 is to be constructed.

  • What should the dimensions of the box be to minimize the surface area of the box?

  • What is the minimum surface area?

  1. String Art Redux. For this problem, I would like you to draw a picture that would result from placing a peg at the positive \(x\) and \(y\) values \([0, 6]\). Draw a line between \(x\) and \(y\) values that add to 6. For example from \(x = 1\) and \(y = 5\).

  • After drawing the figure, we want to describe these lines with an equation. Write two equations, for the lines through \((1, 0), (0, 5)\) and \((2, 0), (0, 4)\).

  • Now, we aim to generalize these equations. Consider the relationship between the \(x\)-intercepts and \(y\)-intercepts; noted as \(a\) and \(b\) respectively.

\[b = 6 - a\]

We use this to write the equation below. This represents any line between \((a, 0)\) and \((0, b)\), your job is to explain how to get here!

\[\frac{x}{a} + \frac{y}{6 - a} = 1\]
